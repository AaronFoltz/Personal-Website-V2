The Heroku instructions with the custom domains app isn't the best, especially if you're trying to work with FatCow. First things first, you need to 

heroku addons:add custom_domains

in order to add the free addon to your heroku app.  You then need to 

heroku domains:add myherokuapp.example.com

in order to specify the subdomain which you want to use with your Heroku app. Everything is now done on the Heroku end, now you just need to setup everything on the FatCow side (this is the bad part, FatCow doesn't seem to have much documentation for anything whatsoever).

  1. Start by logging into your control panel at FatCow.com
  2. Go to http://members.fatcow.com/webControl/domaincentral/beta/index.bml in order to get to the correct section of your dashboard
  3. Click the plus (+) sign next to the desired domain
  4. Click on the "DNS" button that just appeared
  5. On the drop-down selector, choose "CNAME alias"

    6.  

  6. Fill in the correct information for "Host" and "Points To" and then click Add
    • The "Host" is the subdomain that you intend to use.  For instance, my HackerNews api can be found at hackernews.aaronfoltz.com, so my Host is just "hackernews."
    • "Points To" is of course, what you want that subdomain to point to.  The Heroku documentation recommends that you point to "proxy.heroku.com", but I have had luck pointing directly to the application's Heroku URL (myherokuapp.herokuapp.com).

To see if the change has taken effect, run host subdomain.domain.com.  You should see something similar to this: If you don't see this, please give it time.  It can take many hours for you to actually see the changes.
